President-elect Jair Bolsonaro of Brazil has pitched himself as an iron-fisted crusader against the country’s soaring crime. The former Army captain’s controversial, pro-dictatorship rhetoric — he has endorsed torture and favors giving police license to kill — has polarized this country and especially divided women.
We went to Rio de Janeiro to witness the tension in the days leading up to the election and to see how Mr. Bolsonaro, by exploiting fears of crime, has become viewed by many as an advocate for women.
Our video also takes a closer look at the aggressive policing that already takes place inside Rio’s favelas, and how Mr. Bolsonaro might amplify these efforts while in office.
